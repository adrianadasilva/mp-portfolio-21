Photography basics

For my photography project I used my mobile phone. To be able to mimic some of the camera features I downloaded an app called Obscura which let me control the ISO, focus and shutter speed.

I decided on a nature theme for my portfolio, as itâ€™s something that fascinates me and I also had easy access to beaches and parks. This obviously came with its challenges - I had to rely on natural lighting and was dependant on weather conditions.

To edit my pictures I used the free software Ashampoo and adjusted the brightness, contrast and saturation. I also reduced the size of the pictures on Photopea so they would be optimised for web design.

I took photograph one from a low angle to capture the sky as a backdrop but also the different shadows created by the sun which reflects off the leaves. For my second photograph I lowered the ISO and used a fast setting for the shutter speed to freeze motion and create a high contrast between the flames and the background. On photograph three I used again a low angle and manipulated the aperture to a smaller f/number. This created a larger aperture size and thus resulting in a shallow depth of field (Neuberger, 2018). For photograph four I decided to use the window as a frame to the ocean to add interest. And for photograph five I used a macro add-on lens to capture a close shot of the water droplets on the leaf.

Neuberger, L. (2018) Intro To Digital Photography [pdf] Available at: https://dspace.sunyconnect.suny.edu/handle/1951/70367 [Accessed 15 April 2021]
